I initally started at the company with little to no knowledge of how databases work, how enterprise software is developed, or anything in between. Throughout my tenure with the company, however, I not only learned first-hand the ways of professional software development but also I was able to quickly enhance my technical skills in writing clean, testable, and scalable software. Eventually I was able work my way up to making architectural changes to the logic behind the financial software that my team was responsible with developing.

University of Louisville Campus Housing

Residential Area Technology Specialist (RATS)

(August 2017 - March 2018)

While working of University of Louisville Campus Housing, I was able to experience how managing and solving technical problems on a large, corporate scale is properly done. As a Technology Specialist, my colleagues and I had a main goal of providing quality technical support to not only dorms and the students in them but also the UofL Campus Housing staff. We were responsible with managing the hardware in the campus dorms as well as throughout the campus housing staff. This included managing and tracking hardware such as laptops, projectors, printers, and everything in between. On top of that I became familiarized with corporate IT software to help clean and format computers. This job greatly enhanced my ability to trobleshoot and problem solve on a technical level, and it has helped me with my other work both on the professional level and scholastic level.
